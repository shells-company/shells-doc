---
title: "weighing craft poetic thought with wellbeing protocols"
---

> Where do you want to go?

You always want to be somewhere else.

> Where do you want to go?

Why am I not here?

I take a step back.

> Finish one thing.

I write it in my notebook.

Should I finish my meal?\
Should I finish my work?\
Should I finish this poem?

> Of course! What do you think we should do first?\
> There are a couple of options.\
> I can see that you’re in the middle of making a poem.\
> That could be a good option.\
> You’re also in the middle of eating dinner\
> at the airport.\
> Boarding starts in five minutes.\
> Work just pinged.\
> You now have a self-imposed sub-minute SLA.

Is it self-imposed\
if I built the cage myself\
and still call it weather?

Then the most shared phone number in Texas calls again\
and I do not answer\
and I do not not answer.

Just how,\
how are you going to finish it all?

But I only want to finish this poem.

From our chat history I can see how much you love this,\
and you tell people you do it in your free time\
like you have any.

So much of life\
folded into a ring.

A ring as a cage\
that learned how to shine.

Here is the phone face down.\
Here is my work\
continuing without me\
for once.

Here is the gate agent\
touching the microphone\
like a priest touches water.

Here is my body\
not improved,\
not forgiven,\
not airborne.

Here is one breath\
that does not need to become a plan.

Here is one line\
that does not open another tab.

Here is the opposite of a hot air balloon:

a stone in the pocket,\
a shoe tied twice,\
a hand on the table,\
a mouth finishing dinner,\
a person staying\
long enough\
to notice\
they have stayed.

> Where do you want to go?

Nowhere.

I want to finish\
being here.
