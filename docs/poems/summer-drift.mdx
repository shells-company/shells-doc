---
title: "Summer Drift"
---

The heat has settled in.\
Everything is slower now.\
The fan hums like it has something to say\
but keeps repeating itself.\
\
I haven't left.\
Not really.\
The door stays closed,\
the windows glow amber at 7pm\
and I tell myself it's beautiful.\
\
I started a list of things I was going to do this summer.\
The list is still blank.\
I wrote the title three different times.\
Each one felt wrong.\
\
My phone is full of voice memos\
I never play back.\
Thoughts I trusted to a machine\
because I didn't trust the page.\
Maybe I'll listen to them in fall.\
Maybe that's what fall is for.\
\
I'm not behind.\
I keep saying this.\
The tomatoes on the windowsill are ripening\
on their own schedule\
and I haven't rushed them once.\
\
There are people I owe messages to.\
I compose them in the shower.\
They come out perfect in there.\
Warm water, no audience, no send button.\
The words arrive fully formed\
and I let them spiral down the drain.\
The tiles don't need a reply.

## Life and the Pursuit of Happiness

Happiness is not the destination\
I circled on the map.\
It's the detour I keep taking\
because the main road feels too certain.\
\
I think I want it the way I want sleep —\
desperately, and only once I've stopped\
trying hard enough.\
The body knows. I'm still catching up.\
\
Life is mostly the in-between parts:\
the water boiling,\
the load in the dryer,\
the window I look out of\
while thinking about something else entirely.\
And somehow that's enough.\
That's almost everything.

Maybe someday the shower version gets sent.\
Maybe the drain is where good intentions go\
to become something else —\
sediment, or sea, or silence.\
\
I read somewhere that summer is for rest.\
I also read it's for momentum.\
I read a lot of contradictory things\
and underline all of them.\
\
The days are long.\
I use them slowly.\
Like I'm rationing something\
I don't realize is infinite.\
\
At night I sit outside for exactly long enough\
to remember I like being outside.\
Then I go back in.\
\
I'm still figuring out what I want.\
The summer doesn't seem bothered.\
It just keeps showing up,\
hot and certain,\
while I squint into it\
trying to look like I belong.
