This collection is a personal, ongoing series of poems that document inner life — grief, growth, avoidance, and quiet self-discovery. Each piece reads like a fragment of a larger conversation the writer is having with himself.

## Themes

- **Fragmentation & transitions** — Letting go of old selves, friendships, and phases of life
- **Self-reflection** — Looking inward (and sometimes literally in the mirror) to understand identity
- **Avoidance & return** — Procrastinating on hard things, then slowly circling back
- **Domesticity as metaphor** — Cleaning, taxes, razors, plants, and delivery receipts as stand-ins for deeper reckoning
- **Rest vs. momentum** — The tension between stillness and forward motion

## Poems

| Poem | Summary |
| --- | --- |
| **In Pieces** | The disorientation of graduation — friends scattering, identity unmooring. The question of whether to follow a fragment or find your own place. |
| **fragments-two / In 4 Pieces / No more pieces** | Variations on the same core poem, exploring the same moment of dissolution from slightly different angles. |
| **mirror** | Standing in front of a mirror and finally liking what you see. Self-care as metaphor — bees, hives, posture, and the unsettling question: _what do I do when I'm not broken?_ |
| **Spring Cleaning** | A rainy weekend inside, doing taxes, listening to audiobooks, and confronting a backlog of unread emails and unacknowledged feelings. Ends with a toilet as therapist. |
| **Summer Drift** | The heat of summer as a holding pattern — blank lists, unplayed voice memos, shower-written messages that spiral down the drain. Happiness as a detour, not a destination. |
| **Look Up** | A note dated 02/17/2026 leads down a rabbit hole the writer refuses to enter. Dirty nails, duplicate nail kits, and razors pile up — a meditation on not noticing what's already there. |
| **Sarah** | A departure from verse — a raw set of notes from a conversation about documentation workflows, AI tools, and shipping. Sits in the collection as a found poem of work life. |

## Reading Order

These poems don't demand a strict sequence, but reading them in the order they appear traces an arc: from **rupture** (_In Pieces_) → **avoidance** (_Spring Cleaning_) → **drift** (_Summer Drift_) → **self-recognition** (_mirror_) → **presence** (_Look Up_).
