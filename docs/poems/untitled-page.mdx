_In the spirit of ABBA's "Dancing Queen"_

---

> You can dance,\
> You can jive,\
> Having the time of your life —

Ooh, see that patty, watch that scene,\
Diggin' the Burger King.

> Friday night and the fryers are hot,\
> Looking out for a place to eat,\
> Anywhere it smells like a lot,\
> Moving around to the beat.

But I'm the Burger King.\
Old and broken.\
I am so exhausted.

> **You are the Burger King, young and mean,**\
> **Only seventeen.**\
> **Burger King, feel the grease, feel the sheen —**\
> **Oh yeah.**

---

Dairy Queen is old and grey.\
Only sixty-seven.

> Dairy Queen, with her soft-serve crown,\
> Watching empires melt slowly down.\
> She knew the taste of sweeter days,\
> Now lost in a frozen, forgotten haze.

But the fryers keep burning, the grease keeps on singing,\
And somewhere a teenager's order bell ringing.

> **You are the Burger King, young and mean,**\
> **Only seventeen.**\
> **Burger King, feel the grease, feel the sheen —**\
> **Oh yeah.**

---

And the Dairy Queen smiles from across the street,\
Two crowns in a world that runs on repeat.\
Old and broken, young and loud —\
Just two monarchs lost in the fast-food crowd.
